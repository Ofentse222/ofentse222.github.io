5 Free Zapier Alternatives That Actually Work in 2026

Published April 22, 2026 · Automation & Productivity

Zapier is the gold standard for no-code automation, connecting thousands of apps and letting you build workflows without writing a single line of code. The problem? Its free plan allows only 5 Zaps and 100 tasks per month — far too little for anyone serious about automation.

The good news is that several powerful alternatives offer genuinely generous free tiers. Whether you're automating your business, your side project, or your personal workflow, at least one of these tools will fit your needs without costing you anything.

What to Look for in a Zapier Alternative

Key factors include how many automated workflows you get for free, how many tasks run per month, which apps are supported, and how complex your automations can be. Some tools allow multi-step workflows on the free plan — something Zapier reserves for paid tiers.

The 5 Best Free Alternatives

1. Make (formerly Integromat) Best Overall

Make is the most powerful free Zapier alternative available. Its visual workflow builder uses a drag-and-drop canvas that lets you see your entire automation mapped out as a diagram. The free plan includes 1,000 operations per month and allows unlimited scenarios. You can build multi-step workflows, use conditional logic, and connect to over 1,500 apps.

Free plan: 1,000 operations/month · Unlimited scenarios · 1,500+ apps

2. n8n Best for Tech Users

n8n is an open-source automation platform that you can self-host for completely free, unlimited usage. It supports over 400 integrations and allows highly complex workflows with loops, conditional branches, and custom code nodes. Self-hosting removes all limits entirely.

Free plan: 5 active workflows (cloud) · Unlimited if self-hosted · 400+ integrations

3. Pabbly Connect Best Value

Pabbly Connect stands out because its pricing model counts internal tasks differently — meaning you often get far more automation power per free task than Zapier. It supports over 1,000 app integrations and allows multi-step workflows on all plans.

Free plan: 100 tasks/month · Unlimited workflows · 1,000+ apps

4. IFTTT Best for Beginners

IFTTT is the simplest automation tool on this list. It uses a straightforward trigger-action format that anyone can understand within minutes. The free plan allows up to 5 applets and connects to a wide range of consumer services including Gmail, Google Drive, and smart home devices.

Free plan: 5 applets · Single-step only · Consumer apps focus

5. Activepieces Best Open Source

Activepieces is a newer open-source platform that has gained rapid popularity as a direct Zapier replacement. Its interface is clean and familiar to Zapier users. The cloud free plan includes 1,000 tasks per month. Self-hosting removes all limits.

Free plan: 1,000 tasks/month (cloud) · Unlimited if self-hosted

Which One Should You Choose?

For most users, Make is the best starting point — it balances power, ease of use, and a genuinely useful free tier. If you need unlimited automations and have basic technical skills, n8n self-hosted is unbeatable. Beginners should start with IFTTT, then graduate to Make when ready for more complexity.

Final Thoughts

Automation is one of the highest-leverage skills you can develop as a freelancer or entrepreneur. The good news is you no longer need to pay Zapier's prices to access serious automation capabilities. Start with Make or IFTTT today — you can build your first automation in under 20 minutes.
