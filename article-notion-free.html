Notion Free Plan: Everything You Get in 2026

Published April 20, 2026 · Productivity

Notion has become one of the most popular productivity tools in the world, and for good reason. It combines note-taking, databases, project management, and wikis all in one place. But with so many features on offer, many people wonder: is the free plan actually useful, or is it just a teaser?

In this article, we break down exactly what you get with Notion's free plan in 2026, what its limitations are, and whether upgrading is worth considering.

What's Included in the Free Plan

Notion's free tier is genuinely generous for individual users. You get unlimited pages and blocks for personal use, meaning you can create as many notes, databases, and documents as you like without hitting a wall. You also get access to all of Notion's core templates, making it easy to set up systems for task management, journaling, goal tracking, and more.

The free plan also includes basic collaboration — you can invite up to 10 guests to view or edit your pages. This makes it workable for small side projects or family use. Additionally, you get 7-day version history, which means you can roll back changes made within the past week.

Key Limitations to Know

The biggest restriction on the free plan is around collaboration. If you're working with a team, the guest limit and lack of team workspaces can become a bottleneck quickly. The free plan does not include advanced permissions, meaning you cannot restrict access at the block level or create role-based team environments.

Version history is limited to 7 days on the free tier. For most casual users this is fine, but if you need to recover older versions of important documents, the paid plans extend this to 30 days or more. File upload limits are also capped at 5MB per file, which can be restrictive if you regularly attach images or documents.

Who Should Stick with the Free Plan

If you're a solo user building personal knowledge bases, managing tasks, or writing notes, Notion's free plan is more than adequate. Students, freelancers working alone, and anyone using Notion purely for personal organisation will find the free tier does everything they need without spending a cent.

Who Should Consider Upgrading

If you're managing a team, need extended version history, or frequently upload large files, the Plus plan at around $10 per month per member becomes attractive. The Business plan adds advanced analytics and SAML SSO for larger organisations. For most individuals, though, the free plan remains excellent value.

Final Verdict

Notion's free plan is one of the strongest free productivity tools available in 2026. It gives you a fully functional workspace with no time limits or artificial content restrictions. If you've been putting off trying Notion because you assumed it would cost money, you can stop hesitating — the free version is the real thing.

For teams, the paid tiers unlock meaningful collaboration features. But for personal use, there's no better free all-in-one workspace available today.
